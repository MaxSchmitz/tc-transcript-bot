# The Two Containers

Every Facebook post has exactly two containers. Understanding what belongs in each, and what each one is responsible for, is the difference between a post that works and one that doesn't.

---

## Container 1: Image Container

The image container is the complete standalone unit. Someone scrolling at full speed should understand the core point from the image alone without reading a single word of the caption.

**Components (most posts):**
- Headline: one pithy, viral, complete claim
- Copy: powerful body text that grabs and holds
- Visual: human-made image or collage reinforcing the point

**The standalone rule:**
Nothing critical should live only in the image that requires the caption to complete it. The image is self-sufficient. If the image requires the caption to make sense, the image failed.

**On the headline:**
Most posts include a headline in the image container. Whether a headline is needed depends on the viral function and the nature of the source material. Some functions lead with visual evidence or copy that carries the full weight without a separate headline. The source material and the function determine this, not a default rule.

---

## Container 2: Caption Container

The caption adds depth. It does not complete a thought the image left unfinished.

The caption assumes the image has already done its job and builds on top of it. It can repeat key information to ensure the narrative works — repetition in service of coherence is not redundancy.

**Components:**
- Viral hook (required when the image container has no headline)
- Full context: names, dates, sources, evidence
- Attribution and authority signals
- Supporting evidence
- Extended explanation where needed

**The depth rule:**
The caption adds, it doesn't rescue. If removing the caption would make the post incomprehensible, the image container failed its job.

---

## Universal Principles

**Image complete, caption deep.**
The image carries the point. The caption carries the proof, the context, and the detail that makes someone trust it and share it.

**Repetition is allowed.**
The caption can restate key information from the image when doing so keeps the narrative coherent for readers who engage with the caption first or in isolation.

**Hook placement:**
When the image container has a strong headline, the caption can open with context. When the image has no headline, the caption must open with a viral hook that stops the reader before they scroll past.

**Function determines structure.**
The viral function and the source material determine which components appear in the image container and in what form. The two-container architecture is constant. The configuration inside each container is not.
