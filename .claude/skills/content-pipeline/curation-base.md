# Content Curation

Curation is the highest-leverage skill in the pipeline. A perfectly written post about mediocre source material will always lose to a decent post about extraordinary source material. Choosing what to write about matters more than how you write it.

---

## The 3-Second Test

When you first encounter a piece of content, your gut reaction in the first 3 seconds is data. Ask:

1. **Did I have an immediate emotional reaction?** (anger, surprise, sadness, recognition, disgust, awe)
2. **Do I want to tell someone about this?** Not "should I post this" but "do I physically want to text this to someone?"
3. **Can I see the headline already?** If the headline writes itself from the source material, the content is strong.

If the answer to all three is yes, the content is worth pursuing. If you're trying to convince yourself it's good, it's not.

---

## Evaluation Criteria

Rate each piece on these dimensions. A post doesn't need all of them, but it needs at least 2-3 strong ones.

### 1. Specificity of Detail
Does the source contain specific, concrete details (names, numbers, dates, quotes, places) that make the story feel real and verifiable?

- **Strong:** "He gets 16 to 18 minutes on the phone every 3 hours"
- **Weak:** "He has limited phone access"

Content rich in specific detail is easier to write well and more credible to readers.

### 2. Emotional Trigger
What primary emotion does this content provoke? The strongest viral emotions, roughly ranked:

1. **Outrage** -- injustice, hypocrisy, abuse of power
2. **Awe** -- extraordinary achievement, unexpected beauty, scale
3. **Fear** -- credible threat to safety, health, autonomy, privacy
4. **Recognition** -- "this is exactly me" or "I've been saying this"
5. **Schadenfreude** -- powerful person brought low, comeuppance
6. **Sympathy** -- undeserved suffering, especially of the innocent
7. **Nostalgia** -- resurgence of something from the past

Content that hits multiple emotions (outrage + sympathy, schadenfreude + recognition) is stronger than single-emotion content.

### 3. Novelty
Is this genuinely new information, or a new angle on known information?

- **New information:** first interview, leaked document, unpublished data
- **New angle:** connecting known facts in a way nobody has before
- **Neither:** rehashing something already widely covered with no new take

"Neither" is an automatic pass. Don't post it.

### 4. Conflict or Tension
Does the source contain inherent tension?

- Person vs. institution (Weinstein vs. justice system)
- Claim vs. evidence (he says X, 80 women say Y)
- Before vs. after (laundromat vending machine to $50M company)
- Expectation vs. reality (teacher salary vs. coat expense)

Tension is the engine of engagement. Content without tension requires exceptional specificity or emotion to compensate.

### 5. Shareability Signal
Who specifically would share this, and why?

- **Identity sharing:** "This is so me" (personality types, generational experiences)
- **Outrage sharing:** "Can you believe this?" (injustice, hypocrisy)
- **Utility sharing:** "You need to know this" (health, safety, money)
- **Social currency sharing:** "I found this first" (exclusive info, hidden pattern)

If you can't name the specific audience and their motivation to share, the content will underperform.

---

## Red Flags (Pass on These)

- **No primary source.** If you can't trace the claim back to a document, study, interview, or named person, don't post it.
- **Already saturated.** If 5+ major pages have already posted about this, you're late. The exception: you have a genuinely new angle nobody else took.
- **Requires too much context.** If the reader needs 3 paragraphs of background before the headline makes sense, the content is too niche for a general audience.
- **Outrage without evidence.** Emotional content without verifiable facts erodes trust over time. Every claim must be sourceable.
- **Single-detail stories.** If the entire post rests on one fact and there's no supporting detail to build around it, the post will feel thin.

---

## Priority Ranking

When you have multiple pieces of content competing for attention, rank by:

1. **Exclusivity** -- first interviews, leaked documents, original data always win
2. **Emotional intensity x specificity** -- strong emotion backed by specific detail
3. **Timeliness** -- content connected to something happening right now
4. **Evergreen potential** -- content that will still work in 2 weeks (lower priority but valuable for scheduling)

---

## Quick Decision Framework

| Signal | Action |
|--------|--------|
| Gut reaction + specific details + clear headline | Write it now |
| Gut reaction but thin on details | Check if Grok enrichment can fill gaps. If yes, proceed. If no, pass. |
| Interesting but no emotional trigger | Pass unless the novelty is extraordinary |
| Strong emotion but no verifiable source | Pass. Find the source first or don't post. |
| Good content but already widely covered | Pass unless you have a genuinely different angle |
